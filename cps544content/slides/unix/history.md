- 1969: First UNIX implementation developed by Ken Thompson at Bell Labs (AT&T)
- Initially the kernel was written in assembly
- 1973: C matured enough that the kernel was rewritten in C
- 1971 - 1975: First to sixth editions of UNIX brought increased popularity

---

### History of UNIX

- AT&T monopoly: U.S. government prevented AT&T from selling software
- Instead licensed/gave to Universities. Professors became UNIX evangelists
- 1975: Ken Thompson spent a year a visiting faculty member at UC Berkeley (Cal)
- 1979: Berkeley Standard Distribution (BSD) and System V were released
- BSD became NetBSD and FreeBSD
- Solaris, HP-UX, NeXTStep, IBM AIX

---

### History of GNU

- Initial UNIX implementations were incumbered by patents and licensing
- Richard Stallman believed software should be free and open
- 1985: Founded GNU: "GNU's Not UNIX" (GNU) and Free Software Foundation (FSF)
- GNU General Public License (GPL)
- GNU compiler collection (gcc)

---

### History of Linux

- 1991: Linus Torvalds, as a student, was inspired by Minix (UNIX kernel)
- Wanted to make a full featured UNIX kernel based on Minix
- A few months later released version 0.02 and requested help from the community
- Later the kernel was renamed to Linux, combining Linus but ending traditionally in an "x"
- Most popular open source kernel today

---

### History of POSIX

- Portable Operating System Interface (POSIX) is an IEEE standard in 1988
- Name suggested by Richard Stallman
- 1989: C standardized
- 2017: Latest POSIX revision is IEEE Std 1003.1-2017
- Governs shells, filesystems, threading, I/O

Notes:

- Pronounced "paw six"

---

### Compliance

- Implementation first, standardization second
- Fully POSIX compliant: macOS, AIX, HP-UX, ...
- Mostly POSIX compliant: Linux, NetBSD, and FreeBSD

---

### UNIX Philosophy

#### As redefined by Peter H. Salus

- Write programs that do one thing and do it well.
- Write programs to work together.
- Write programs to handle text streams, because that is a universal interface.
